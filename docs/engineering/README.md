## Engineering question

> **How do we build trustworthy intelligent systems through a disciplined, evidence-centered engineering process that produces reviewable decisions, reusable knowledge, and operational confidence?**

The Engineering Platform answers that question by organizing engineering work into stages. Each stage asks one focused engineering question, produces evidence, and includes a readiness gate before the next stage begins.

---

## What is the Engineering Platform?

The ETIS Engineering Platform is the operational implementation of the ETIS Framework.

It turns the principles of trustworthy intelligent systems into a practical engineering lifecycle. The platform helps teams move from early intent to operational stewardship while preserving the evidence needed to understand what was built, why it was built, how it was verified, how it was released, and how it should evolve.

<div class="etis-card-grid-2">
  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>Repository-centered</h3>
    <p>The repository is the system of record for intent, requirements, decisions, designs, risks, evidence, releases, operations, and stewardship.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>Evidence-centered</h3>
    <p>Important engineering work leaves durable evidence so future engineers, reviewers, instructors, and stakeholders can understand what happened and why.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>Stage-based</h3>
    <p>Engineering Stages guide teams through a sequence of questions that progressively reduce uncertainty and improve decision quality.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>Stewardship-oriented</h3>
    <p>The lifecycle does not end at deployment. It continues through monitoring, learning, improvement, governance, and next-cycle planning.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Engineering philosophy

The Engineering Platform is built on a small set of working commitments.

<div class="etis-card-grid-2">
  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>Engineering before implementation</h3>
    <p>Implementation should begin only after the team understands the problem, requirements, constraints, architecture, design, risks, and readiness expectations.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>One engineering question per stage</h3>
    <p>Each stage focuses attention on one primary question so the team can make progress without hiding uncertainty.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>Everything important leaves evidence</h3>
    <p>Important decisions, reviews, risks, tests, releases, incidents, and lessons should be preserved in the repository.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>Readiness gates govern progression</h3>
    <p>Teams advance when the evidence supports moving forward, not merely because the next activity is scheduled.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>AI may assist; engineers remain accountable</h3>
    <p>AI can help draft, review, summarize, and inspect. Engineers remain responsible for judgment, verification, acceptance, and stewardship.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>Stewardship completes the lifecycle</h3>
    <p>Trustworthy systems require learning after release. Operational evidence must shape future engineering work.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## Recommended workflow

Use the Engineering Platform as a guided engineering process, not as a documentation checklist.

<div class="etis-flow-panel">
<pre><code>Understand the platform
      ↓
Begin ES-100
      ↓
Work each Engineering Stage
      ↓
Produce project evidence
      ↓
Review the readiness gate
      ↓
Advance, return, or revise
      ↓
Operate, monitor, learn, and steward</code></pre>
</div>

<div class="etis-card-grid-2">
  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>1. Start with ES-100</h3>
    <p>ES-100 establishes the journey, principles, stage map, glossary, and readiness model for the platform.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>2. Complete stages intentionally</h3>
    <p>Move through the lifecycle in order unless you are deliberately adapting ETIS to an existing project.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>3. Use templates as starting points</h3>
    <p>Templates help structure evidence. They do not replace engineering judgment or review.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>4. Compare against examples</h3>
    <p>Reference examples show how completed evidence can look, but your project evidence must reflect your actual system.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>5. Respect readiness gates</h3>
    <p>Each readiness gate asks whether enough evidence exists to continue responsibly.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>6. Preserve learning</h3>
    <p>Operations, monitoring, incidents, feedback, and risks should feed stewardship and the next engineering cycle.</p>
  </div>

The Engineering Platform groups fifteen Engineering Stages into four major lifecycle movements: start, frame, build, and operate. Each stage produces evidence and includes a readiness gate before moving forward.

## Before you begin

<div class="etis-card-grid-2">
  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>Stages are sequential by default</h3>
    <p>For a new project, complete ES-100 through ES-114 in order. For an existing project, enter at the earliest stage where evidence is weak or missing.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>Evidence belongs in the repository</h3>
    <p>Do not leave important decisions in chat, memory, email, or tool output only. The repository is the engineering record.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>Templates are not proof</h3>
    <p>A filled template is useful only when it reflects real engineering work and supports reviewable judgment.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="etis-product-card">
    <h3>Readiness gates are decisions</h3>
    <p>Each gate asks whether the team can responsibly move forward, needs correction, or must return to an earlier stage.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="etis-ui-callout warning">
  <strong>Common Pitfall</strong>
  <p>Do not treat the Engineering Platform as a checklist to finish quickly. The purpose is to improve engineering judgment and preserve evidence, not accelerate paperwork.</p>
</div>

<div class="etis-ui-callout insight">
  <strong>Engineering Promise</strong>
  <p>If every Engineering Stage is completed honestly and every readiness gate is respected, the resulting repository will explain not only what the system does, but why it was built, how engineering decisions were made, how trust was established, and how future engineers should continue its stewardship.</p>
</div>
