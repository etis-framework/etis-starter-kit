# ETIS Design Philosophy

## Design Identity

ETIS products should feel like professional engineering systems.

They should not feel like generic documentation, academic handouts, marketing pages, or template dumps. They should feel like a trusted engineering workbench.

## Desired User Reaction

A first-time visitor should think:

- I understand what this is.
- I know where to start.
- This feels serious and professional.
- This is more than documentation.
- This platform will guide me through disciplined engineering work.

## Tone

ETIS design should communicate:

- clarity;
- engineering seriousness;
- calm confidence;
- practical guidance;
- professional trust;
- intellectual rigor.

## What ETIS Design Should Avoid

ETIS pages should avoid:

- decorative clutter;
- unnecessary animation;
- excessive visual noise;
- unstructured walls of text;
- unlabeled navigation;
- pages that start without context;
- pages that end without a next step;
- inconsistent callout styling;
- inconsistent headings and metadata.

## Visual Personality

ETIS visual identity should remain close to the existing language:

- deep navy foundation;
- gold emphasis;
- blue action color;
- white content cards;
- clean typography;
- restrained gradients;
- engineering-grid feel;
- card-based navigation;
- professional rather than playful.

## Design Rule

Every visual element must serve one of these purposes:

1. orientation;
2. navigation;
3. comprehension;
4. decision support;
5. evidence discovery;
6. action.
